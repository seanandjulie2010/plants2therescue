Why this works

This bowl is big flavor with small effort. Steaming kale makes it tender and sweet instead of bitter. Warm chickpeas add comfort and protein. The lemon-tahini sauce acts like a creamy dressing without oil, clinging to every leaf and bean so each bite tastes bright, savory, and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients (Serves 2)

Main Bowl

  • 1 large bunch kale (any kind), stems removed, leaves chopped
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas, drained and rinsed

Instructions

Before you start (1 minute)

  • Rinse and drain the chickpeas.
  • Wash the kale, remove thick stems, and chop leaves into bite-size pieces.
  • Finely mince or grate the garlic.
  • Cut the lemon and set it aside.

1) Steam the kale (no oil)

  • Place a large skillet over medium heat.
  • Add chopped kale and 2‚Äì3 tbsp water.
  • Cover and steam 3‚Äì4 minutes.
  • Stir once halfway through.
  • Add 1 tbsp water if the pan looks dry.

2) Warm the chickpeas

  • Keep kale in the pan.
  • Add chickpeas and about 2 tbsp water.
  • Warm gently for 2 minutes.

3) Add the garlic

  • Lower heat to low.
  • Add garlic and stir for 30 seconds.
  • Turn off heat.

4) Make the sauce

  • Whisk tahini, lemon juice, and salt.
  • Add warm water 1 tbsp at a time until creamy and pourable.

5) Combine

  • Pour sauce over kale and chickpeas.
  • Toss gently to coat.
