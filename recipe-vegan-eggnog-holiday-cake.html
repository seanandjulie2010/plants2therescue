Vegan Eggnog Holiday Cake

Vegan • Whole-Food Ingredients

Prep: 15–20 min Bake: 32–38 min Pan: 9-inch round cake
← Back to All Recipes

Moist, cozy, warmly spiced vegan eggnog cake with cashew cream frosting, perfect for Christmas or winter gatherings.

Why this works

Vegan eggnog becomes the heart of this cake—bringing gentle holiday spice, creamy richness, and a nostalgic Christmas flavor. Oat and almond flours keep it tender and wholesome, while applesauce and dates replace oil and refined sugar for a naturally sweet, moist crumb.

Ingredients

Dry

  • 2 cups oat flour (or blend rolled oats into flour)
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp allspice
  • ¼ tsp salt (optional)

Wet

  • 1 ¼ cups vegan eggnog
  • ¾ cup unsweetened applesauce
  • ½ cup date paste or ½ cup blended Medjool dates
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar

Eggnog Cashew Cream Frosting (Optional, but so good)

  • 1 cup raw cashews (soaked in hot water 20 mins, then drained)
  • ⅓–½ cup vegan eggnog (as needed for consistency)
  • 3–4 pitted dates
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • Pinch of nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 9-inch round cake pan with parchment (bottom and, if you like, the sides).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together all dry ingredients until the spices are evenly distributed.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the wet ingredients: vegan eggnog, applesauce, date paste, vanilla, and apple cider vinegar.
  4. Pour the wet mixture into the dry. Fold gently until just combined. The batter should be thick but pourable—avoid overmixing.
  5. Pour batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  6. Bake 32–38 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean or with just a few soft crumbs.
  7. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10–15 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling is very important for oil-free cakes so they set and slice cleanly.

Eggnog Cashew Cream Frosting

  1. Soak cashews in hot water for 20 minutes, then drain and rinse.
  2. Add soaked cashews, ⅓ cup vegan eggnog, dates, vanilla, and nutmeg to a blender.
  3. Blend until silky smooth, adding a little more eggnog as needed to reach a thick, spreadable consistency.
  4. Chill 30–60 minutes to set up before spreading on the cooled cake.

Serving Suggestions

  • Dust the top with fresh nutmeg.
  • Add a sprinkle of cinnamon for extra warmth.
  • Garnish with fresh cranberries or toasted pecans (if not avoiding nuts) for a festive Christmas look.
  • Serve with a small glass of chilled vegan eggnog.

Notes

  • For gluten-free, be sure your oat flour is certified gluten-free.
  • If your vegan nog is already sweetened, you can reduce the date paste slightly.
  • Batter can also be baked as cupcakes (18–22 minutes) or a loaf (40–50 minutes)Start checking a bit early.
