Why this works

Traditional Peruvian chocotejas are filled chocolate treats with a caramel-like center. This whole-food version uses kidney beans for structure and creaminess, and dates for a naturally sweet, fudgy truffle texture without any sugar or oil. With cinnamon, vanilla, and a tiny coffee boost, these taste like a rich chocolate dessert from a bakery.

Instructions

1) Prep the kidney beans (important)

  • Rinse the kidney beans extremely well.
  • Rinse again (seriously).
  • Pat dry with a paper towel.
  • Let them air dry for about 10 minutes.
  • This removes the ‚Äúbean taste‚Äù and makes the truffles taste like pure chocolate.

2) Blend into truffle dough

  • Add kidney beans, dates, cocoa powder, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt to a food processor.
  • Add instant coffee + cloves if using.
  • Blend until thick, smooth, and fudge-like.
  • If needed, add 1 tbsp water (or orange juice) at a time to help it blend.

3) Add mix-ins (optional)

  • Stir in walnuts, raisins, coconut, or other mix-ins if desired.

4) Chill

  • Place the mixture in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  • This makes it easier to roll into perfect truffles.

5) Roll + coat

  • Scoop about 1 tablespoon per truffle.
  • Roll into balls.
  • Roll each truffle in cocoa, coconut, walnuts, or your coating of choice.
