Hearty Lentil & Mushroom Shepherd’s Pie

Vegan • Oil-Free • Sugar-Free • Nut-Free

This shepherd’s pie is classic comfort food made with whole-food ingredients: lentils, mushrooms, vegetables and a fluffy potato topping—no oil, no dairy.

Ingredients (4 servings)

Lentil & Veggie Base

  • 1 cup dry brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 3 cups low-sodium vegetable broth or water
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cups chopped mushrooms
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste (no added sugar)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt-free seasoning blend or a pinch of salt if you use it

Potato Topping

  • 5–6 medium potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2–3/4 cup unsweetened soy, oat or other nut-free plant milk
  • Freshly ground black pepper (optional)
  • Salt-free seasoning blend or a pinch of salt if you use it

Instructions

  1. Cook the potatoes: Add potatoes to a pot, cover with water and boil until very tender (about 15–20 minutes). Drain and set aside.
  2. Cook the lentils: In a separate pot, combine lentils and vegetable broth or water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook until tender and most of the liquid is absorbed (about 20–25 minutes).
  3. Cook the vegetables: In a large pan, add onion, carrots, celery and mushrooms with a splash of water or broth. Sauté, stirring often, until the vegetables soften and the mushrooms release their juices (about 8–10 minutes). Add small splashes of water as needed to prevent sticking.
  4. Combine base: Stir the cooked lentils into the pan with the vegetables. Add peas, tomato paste, thyme, rosemary, garlic and seasoning. If mixture seems dry, add 1/4–1/2 cup water or broth. Simmer for 5 minutes, then spread evenly into a baking dish.
  5. Make the mashed potatoes: Mash the cooked potatoes with the plant milk and seasoning until fluffy. Adjust thickness with more plant milk if needed.
  6. Top and bake: Spread the mashed potatoes over the lentil mixture. Rough up the top with a fork for texture. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20–25 minutes, until the top is lightly golden in spots and the filling is bubbling at the edges.
  7. Serve: Let it cool for 5–10 minutes, then scoop and enjoy.

Tips

  • Use frozen mixed vegetables instead of chopping everything if you’re short on time.
  • Leftovers keep well in the fridge for up to 3–4 days and reheat nicely.
  • For extra flavor, add a splash of balsamic vinegar or tamari (if you use it) to the lentil base.
