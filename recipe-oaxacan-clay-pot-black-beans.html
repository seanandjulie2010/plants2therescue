Why this works

These beans taste rich without oil because the flavor is built the traditional way: dry charring onion, garlic, and chile for smokiness, then slow simmering with epazote and herbs until the broth turns velvety. Salt goes in at the end to keep the beans tender and creamy.

Ingredients (4 servings)

Beans + Base

  • 1 cup dry black beans, rinsed (no soak needed)
  • 5 cups water (plus more as needed)

Charred aromatics (oil-free)

  • 1/2 white onion, cut in half
  • 3 cloves garlic, unpeeled
  • 1 dried pasilla chile (or ancho), stem removed

Herbs + spice

  • 1 tsp dried epazote (or 1 fresh sprig)
  • 1/4 tsp Mexican oregano, crushed
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • Optional: 1/4 tsp smoked paprika

Finish

  • 3/4‚Äì1 tsp sea salt (add at the end)
  • Optional: 1 small strip orange peel (remove before serving)
  • Optional toppings: cilantro, hoja santa, lime wedges

Instructions

1) Dry-char the aromatics (the flavor builder)

  • Heat a dry skillet (cast iron is perfect) over medium-high.
  • Char onion halves (cut-side down), garlic (unpeeled), and the dried chile until blistered and fragrant.
  • This replaces oil with a deep smoky flavor.

2) Start the simmer

  • Add beans and water to a pot (clay pot if you have one).
  • Add the charred onion, garlic, and chile.
  • Stir in epazote, oregano, cumin, and smoked paprika (if using).
  • Optional: add a strip of orange peel for a subtle floral lift.

3) Slow-cook until creamy

  • Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a bare simmer.
  • Cover loosely and cook 90‚Äì120 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • If the water drops below the beans, add a splash of hot water as needed.

4) Remove aromatics + season

  • Remove onion, garlic skins, chile, and orange peel (if used).
  • Now add salt (adding it late helps the beans stay tender).

5) Serve

  • Serve with warm corn tortillas, lime, and chopped cilantro.
  • Perfect alongside calabacitas, saut√©ed greens (water/broth saut√©), or nopales.

Helpful Notes

  • No epazote? Use a bay leaf plus extra oregano (different, but still great).
  • Want thicker broth? Mash 1/4 cup beans into the pot and stir.
  • Want it smokier? Add a pinch of chipotle powder or keep the chile in longer.

Serving ideas

  • Classic: bowl of beans, tortillas, lime, cilantro.
  • Meal prep: serve over brown rice or quinoa with grilled peppers/onions (dry pan + broth).
  • Tostadas: smash beans, spread on baked tostadas, top with pico and shredded cabbage.
