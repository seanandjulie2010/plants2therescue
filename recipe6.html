Simple Baked Potato & Greens Plate

Vegan • Oil-Free • Sugar-Free • Nut-Free

A minimalist “reset” meal: a fluffy baked potato and tender seasoned greens. Easy, grounding and budget-friendly.

Ingredients (1 serving)

  • 1 large russet or sweet potato
  • 2 cups chopped kale, collards or spinach
  • 2–3 tablespoons water or vegetable broth (for sautéing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt-free seasoning blend or a pinch of salt if you use it
  • Lemon wedge for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bake the potato: Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Pierce the potato a few times with a fork and bake for 45–50 minutes, until tender. For a faster version, microwave for 7–8 minutes, turning halfway through.
  2. Cook the greens: In a pan over medium heat, add the greens and a few tablespoons of water or broth. Sauté, stirring often, until wilted and tender (about 5–7 minutes). Add more water as needed to prevent sticking.
  3. Season: Sprinkle smoked paprika, garlic powder and seasoning over the greens. Stir to combine.
  4. Assemble the plate: Split the baked potato open on a plate and fluff the inside with a fork. Spoon the greens on top or alongside.
  5. Serve: Add a squeeze of lemon if you like and enjoy warm.

Tips

  • Batch-bake several potatoes at once and store them in the fridge for quick meals.
  • Mix and match different greens depending on what’s affordable and available.
  • Add a spoonful of salsa or oil-free hummus on top of the potato for extra flavor.
