Why this works

This is a nourishing remake of the classic strawberry shortcake roll, soft, sweet, and nostalgic, but made with whole-food ingredients. The sponge is gently sweetened with date paste and flavored with vanilla + carob (naturally sweet and caffeine-free). The filling is a creamy strawberry ‚Äúcream‚Äù made from blended cashews and fresh berries, and the outside gets a strawberry crunch coating for that classic snack-cake vibe without oil or refined sugar.

Ingredients (makes 6‚Äì10 rolls)

1) Carob-Vanilla Sponge

  • 1 cup oat flour (or blend rolled oats into flour)
  • 1/4 cup almond flour (optional, for extra softness)
  • 2 tbsp carob powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened plant milk (almond, soy, or oat)
  • 1/4 cup date paste
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 2 1/2 tbsp warm water (flax egg)

2) Strawberry Cream Filling

  • 3/4 cup raw cashews (soaked 4 hours, then drained)
  • 1/2 cup fresh strawberries
  • 2 tbsp date paste
  • 1‚Äì2 tbsp unsweetened plant milk (only if needed to blend)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: squeeze of lemon (brightens strawberry flavor)

3) Strawberry Crunch Coating

  • 1/2 cup freeze-dried strawberries, crushed
  • 1/4 cup rolled oats (pulsed briefly into small crumbs)
  • 1 tbsp carob powder
  • Optional: 1 tbsp date powder (extra sweetness)

Instructions

1) Make the flax egg

  • In a small bowl, mix 1 tbsp ground flax with 2 1/2 tbsp warm water.
  • Let sit for 5 minutes to thicken.

2) Bake the sponge

  • Preheat oven to 350¬∞F.
  • Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  • In a large bowl, whisk: oat flour, almond flour (if using), carob powder, baking powder, and salt.
  • In another bowl, mix: plant milk, date paste, vanilla, and the flax egg.
  • Pour wet into dry and stir until smooth.
  • Spread batter into a thin rectangle (about 1/4 inch thick).
  • Bake for 10‚Äì12 minutes, just until set (don‚Äôt overbake).
  • Let cool completely before filling.

3) Make the strawberry cream

  • Add drained cashews, strawberries, date paste, vanilla, and lemon (optional) to a blender.
  • Blend until completely smooth and thick.
  • If needed, add 1 tbsp plant milk at a time to help blending.
  • Chill for 20‚Äì30 minutes to thicken.

4) Make the strawberry crunch

  • In a bowl, mix crushed freeze-dried strawberries, oat crumbs, carob powder, and date powder (optional).

5) Assemble the rolls

  • Spread the strawberry cream evenly over the cooled sponge.
  • Using the parchment to help, gently roll the sponge from the short end.
  • Place seam-side down and chill for 1 hour to firm.
  • Slice into rolls.
  • Gently press the outside of each roll into the strawberry crunch coating.

Helpful Notes

  • If the sponge cracks: it‚Äôs usually from overbaking. Roll gently and chill‚Äîstill delicious.
  • Nut-free option: replace cashews with soaked sunflower seeds (same amount) for the filling.
  • Sweeter rolls: add 1 tbsp date powder to the crunch coating.
  • Make-ahead: rolls taste even better after chilling overnight.

Serving ideas

  • Snack cake style: serve chilled straight from the fridge.
  • Dessert plate: add fresh strawberries and a dusting of crushed freeze-dried berries.
  • Party tray: slice thinner for bite-size pinwheels.
  • Extra fun: drizzle with a quick carob ‚Äúsyrup‚Äù (carob + warm plant milk + date paste).

Storage

  • Fridge: up to 4 days in a sealed container.
  • Best texture: chilled (the filling firms up nicely).
  • Freezer: freeze sliced rolls on a tray, then store in a container for up to 1 month.
