About this recipe

This creamy green tea pudding highlights matcha as the star ingredient, using whole-food sweetness from dates instead of sugar. Coconut and almond milk make it silky without oil, and gentle heating keeps the matcha flavor smooth, calm, and comforting.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (13.5 oz) unsweetened light coconut milk
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk (or other plant milk)
  • 4‚Äì6 Medjool dates, pitted
  • 2 tbsp matcha green tea powder
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch (or arrowroot powder)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of sea salt (optional)
  • Optional toppings: berries, sliced kiwi, chia or hemp seeds, unsweetened coconut

Instructions

  1. Make a date cream. If the dates are firm, soak them in warm water for 10 minutes to soften, then drain. Blend the dates with 2‚Äì4 tbsp of the almond milk until completely smooth and creamy. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the matcha. Sift or whisk the matcha powder into a small bowl to remove any clumps. Add 2‚Äì3 tbsp warm (not boiling) water and whisk until it becomes a smooth, lump-free paste.
  3. Warm the base. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the coconut milk and the remaining almond milk until uniform. Add the date cream, vanilla, and sea salt (if using). Place over medium heat and warm gently, whisking, until the mixture is hot but not yet simmering.
  4. Add the matcha. Whisk the matcha paste into the warm milk mixture until the color is evenly distributed and the pudding base is a soft green shade.
  5. Make the thickener. In a separate small bowl, whisk the cornstarch with 1/4 cup cool water or plant milk until completely smooth with no lumps. This is your slurry.
  6. Thicken the pudding. While whisking the warm matcha mixture, slowly pour in the cornstarch slurry. Continue to cook over medium heat, stirring constantly for 3‚Äì5 minutes. The pudding will gently bubble and thicken. Avoid a hard boil so the matcha stays smooth and not bitter.
  7. Check the consistency. When the pudding coats the back of a spoon and holds soft swirls on top, remove from heat. It will thicken more as it cools.
  8. Cool and chill. Pour into 3‚Äì4 small bowls or jars. Let cool at room temperature for about 10‚Äì15 minutes, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until fully set and chilled.
  9. Serve. Top with berries, sliced kiwi, coconut, or seeds if desired. Enjoy straight from the fridge as a calm, nourishing dessert or snack.

Notes

  • Sweeter pudding: use 6 dates or add a drizzle of date syrup on top.
  • Thicker texture: add an extra 1 tsp cornstarch.
  • Smoother finish: use an immersion blender before chilling if any tiny lumps remain.
  • Flavor tip: keep the heat gentle ‚Äî matcha is delicate and tastes best without hard boiling.
