Vegan Matcha Green Tea Pudding

Vegan • Oil-Free • Sugar-Free (no refined sugar) • Whole-Food Plant-Based

DessertSnackGreen TeaPlants 2 the Rescue

About This Recipe

This creamy matcha green tea pudding is made with whole-food ingredients and gently sweetened with dates. It’s completely vegan, oil-free, and free from refined sugar, with the star flavor coming from earthy, antioxidant-rich green tea. Enjoy it as a light dessert or a mid-day snack.

Ingredients

For the Pudding

  • 1 can (13.5 oz / 400 ml) unsweetened light coconut milk
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk (or other plant milk)
  • 2 tablespoons matcha green tea powder
  • 4–6 pitted Medjool dates (adjust to taste), soaked in warm water if firm
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch or arrowroot powder
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt (optional)

Optional Toppings

  • Fresh berries
  • Sliced kiwi
  • Chia seeds or hemp hearts
  • Unsweetened shredded coconut

Instructions

  1. Blend the dates. In a blender, combine the dates with 1/2 cup of the almond milk and blend until completely smooth, forming a date cream.
  2. Whisk the matcha. In a small bowl, whisk the matcha with 2–3 tablespoons of warm water until no lumps remain.
  3. Combine base ingredients. In a medium saucepan, add the coconut milk, the remaining almond milk, date cream, vanilla, and optional salt. Whisk in the matcha paste until well combined.
  4. Prepare the thickener. In a small bowl, whisk the cornstarch with 1/4 cup cool almond milk (or water) until smooth and lump-free.
  5. Cook the pudding. Place the saucepan over medium heat. When the mixture is warm, slowly whisk in the cornstarch slurry. Continue to cook, whisking constantly, for 3–5 minutes, or until the pudding thickens and becomes silky. Avoid boiling hard to keep the matcha flavor gentle and smooth.
  6. Chill. Remove from heat. Pour the pudding into 4 small bowls or jars. Let it cool for 10–15 minutes at room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until fully set.
  7. Serve. Top with fresh fruit, seeds, or coconut if desired and enjoy chilled.

Notes & Variations

  • Sweeter pudding: Add an extra date or a few drops of liquid stevia/monk fruit if you use those.
  • Extra creamy: Use full-fat coconut milk and slightly reduce the almond milk.
  • Protein boost: Blend in 1/2 block of silken tofu with the liquids before heating for a higher-protein pudding.
  • Parfait idea: Layer the pudding with berries and puffed whole grains for a Plants 2 the Rescue parfait.
