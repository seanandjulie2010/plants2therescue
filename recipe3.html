Baked Apple Cinnamon Oat Cups

Vegan • Oil-Free • Sugar-Free • Nut-Free

These baked oat cups are like portable apple cinnamon oatmeal—naturally sweetened with fruit, perfect for breakfast or a snack.

Ingredients (10–12 cups)

  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 cup unsweetened soy, oat or other nut-free plant milk
  • 1 medium apple, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup raisins or chopped dates (optional, for extra sweetness)
  • 2 tablespoons ground flaxseed
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder

Instructions

  1. Preheat: Heat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with silicone or paper liners (or use a silicone muffin tray).
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine oats, ground flaxseed, cinnamon and baking powder. Stir to mix.
  3. Add wet ingredients: Stir in applesauce, plant milk and vanilla until the mixture is well combined.
  4. Fold in add-ins: Add the chopped apple and raisins or dates (if using). The batter will be thick and spoonable.
  5. Fill the cups: Spoon the mixture evenly into the muffin cups, pressing down gently so the mixture sticks together.
  6. Bake: Bake for 22–26 minutes, until the tops are set and lightly golden around the edges.
  7. Cool: Allow the oat cups to cool in the pan for at least 10 minutes before removing so they firm up.
  8. Serve: Enjoy warm, or store in the fridge for up to 4–5 days. They also freeze well.

Tips

  • Reheat in the microwave or toaster oven with a splash of plant milk if you like them softer.
  • For more cinnamon flavor, sprinkle extra on top before baking.
  • Leave out the raisins/dates if you want them very low in sweetness—applesauce and apple will still add flavor.
