Chai-Spiced Vegan Cupcakes

Vegan • Oil-Free • Sugar-Free • Chai Forward

Serves: 12 cupcakes
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 18–22 minutes
Features: Whole-food plant-based, date-sweetened, chai-spiced

About This Recipe

These chai-spiced vegan cupcakes are soft, tender, and naturally sweetened with dates — no oil, no refined sugar. The flavor comes from warm chai spices and strong brewed chai tea. Enjoy them plain, or top them with the optional chai cashew cream frosting for an extra-cozy treat.

Ingredients

Dry Ingredients

  • 1 ¾ cups oat flour (or finely ground rolled oats)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cardamom
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon allspice (optional, for deeper chai flavor)

Wet Ingredients

  • 1 cup strong brewed chai tea, cooled (see quick version below)
  • 1 cup pitted Medjool dates (about 10–12), soaked and blended into a paste
  • ½ cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Optional Chai Cashew Cream Frosting

  • 1 cup raw cashews, soaked in hot water for 20 minutes and drained
  • 2–3 tablespoons brewed chai tea
  • 2–3 tablespoons date paste (to taste)
  • Pinch of cinnamon and cardamom
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

Quick Homemade Chai Tea

You can use your favorite chai tea bags, or make a quick version:

  • 2 black tea bags
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4–6 cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • ½ teaspoon grated or sliced fresh ginger (or a pinch of ground)
  • 2 whole cloves

Add everything to a mug or small pot with 1 ½ cups hot water. Steep for about 10 minutes, then strain and cool. Measure 1 cup for the recipe.

Instructions

Make the Cupcakes

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 12-cup muffin pan with parchment or silicone liners.
  2. Prepare the date paste if you haven’t already: soak the pitted dates in hot water for 10–15 minutes, then drain and blend with just enough fresh water to form a smooth, thick paste.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the oat flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, cloves, and allspice until well combined.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk the cooled chai tea, date paste, applesauce, and vanilla until smooth.
  5. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients. Stir gently until just combined. Avoid over-mixing; a few small lumps are okay.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the 12 cupcake liners. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any air bubbles.
  7. Bake for 18–22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center of a cupcake comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs.
  8. Remove the pan from the oven and let the cupcakes cool in the pan for 5–10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely before frosting.

Make the Chai Cashew Cream Frosting (Optional)

  1. Add the soaked and drained cashews, 2 tablespoons chai tea, 2 tablespoons date paste, cinnamon, cardamom, and vanilla to a high-speed blender.
  2. Blend until completely smooth and creamy, stopping to scrape down the sides as needed. Add an extra splash of chai tea only if necessary to help it blend; you want a thick, spreadable frosting.
  3. Taste and adjust sweetness or spice, adding a little more date paste or cinnamon/cardamom if desired.
  4. Transfer to a bowl and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to firm up before piping or spreading on the cooled cupcakes.

Serving & Tips

  • Decorate some cupcakes with frosting and leave others plain, just like in the photo. Both versions are delicious.
  • For a café-style look, dust the frosted cupcakes lightly with cinnamon and top with a single star anise (remove before eating).
  • Store unfrosted cupcakes in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the fridge for up to 5 days. Frosted cupcakes keep best covered in the fridge.
  • These pair perfectly with a mug of warm chai or rooibos tea for a cozy, plant-powered treat.
