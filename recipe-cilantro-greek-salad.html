Why this works

Traditional Greek salads often rely on olive oil for richness. This version stays light and healing by using lemon juice + red wine vinegar for brightness, and a little aquafaba (or water) to soften the acidity and help the herbs cling to the veggies. The cilantro adds a fresh, green lift without overpowering the classic oregano vibe.

Instructions

Step 1) Prepare the optional ‚ÄúFeta-Style‚Äù tofu

Step 2) Prep the vegetables

  • Dice the cucumber into bite-sized pieces.
  • Cut cherry tomatoes in half.
  • Slice red onion very thin.
  • Slice bell pepper into thin strips.
  • Finely chop the cilantro.
  • Add all vegetables + cilantro to a large mixing bowl.

Step 3) Make the oil-free dressing

  • In a small bowl or jar, add: lemon juice, red wine vinegar, aquafaba (or water), oregano, garlic powder, black pepper, and optional salt.
  • Whisk or stir very well until fully combined.
  • Taste it:
    • Add a little more lemon if you want it brighter.
    • Add a splash more water if it feels too strong.

Step 4) Assemble the salad

  • Pour the dressing over the vegetables.
  • Toss gently until everything is evenly coated.
  • Add the marinated tofu (if using).

Step 5) Rest and serve

  • Taste one last time and adjust seasoning if needed.

Helpful Notes

  • The dressing replaces oil with acidity + herbs, so it stays bright and light.
  • Cilantro adds freshness without overpowering the oregano.
