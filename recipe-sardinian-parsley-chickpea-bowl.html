Why this works

This is Sardinia-inspired comfort food built from humble ingredients: herbs, legumes, and bread. Parsley isn‚Äôt just a garnish here; it becomes the broth itself. Blended into a warm, garlicky, lemon bright base, it turns simple chickpeas and rustic flatbread into something deeply satisfying without oil or added sugar.

Ingredients (2 servings)

Base

Instructions

1) Make the parsley broth

  • Add parsley, garlic, bay leaf, and water/broth to a pot.
  • Bring to a gentle simmer and cook 10 minutes.
  • Remove the bay leaf.
  • Blend until completely smooth and bright green.
  • Return to the pot. Season lightly with salt (optional) and black pepper. Keep warm on low.

2) Warm the chickpeas

  • Add chickpeas to a pan with a splash of water.
  • Stir in fennel seed, smoked paprika, lemon juice, and black pepper.
  • Mash about ‚Öì of the chickpeas for a saucier texture.
  • Warm through and set aside.

3) Assemble (Pane Frattau style)

  • Tear flatbread into bite-sized pieces and place in each bowl.
  • Ladle hot parsley broth over the bread to soften it.
  • Spoon chickpeas on top.
  • Optional: repeat one more layer for extra hearty bowls.

4) Finish + serve

  • Top generously with fresh chopped parsley.
  • Add capers if using.
  • Finish with lemon juice or lemon wedges to brighten everything.

Helpful Notes

  • If you want it thicker: use slightly less broth or mash more chickpeas.
  • If parsley tastes ‚Äútoo green‚Äù: add more lemon and a pinch more salt (or skip stems next time).
  • This tastes even better after resting for 5 minutes; everything melds together.

  • If it browns too fast, reduce the heat slightly.
  • Best the same day, but reheats great in a dry skillet.
